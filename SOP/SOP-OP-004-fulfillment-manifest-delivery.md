# SOP-004 — Fulfillment, Manifest, and Delivery Coordination

## Objective

Coordinate order fulfillment and delivery documentation so the physical shipment, ERP order, inventory records, and Metrc transfer information remain aligned.

## Trigger

A validated Oregon sales order is released for fulfillment.

## Procedure

1. **Confirm fulfillment readiness.**
   - Confirm order status is released/approved.
   - Confirm SKU, quantity, customer, delivery date, and ship-to location.
   - Confirm no open pricing, license, AR, or compliance holds.

2. **Coordinate pick/pack with inventory team.**
   - Warehouse confirms picked quantities.
   - Any shortages or substitutions are routed back to Sales Data Coordinator and Sales Rep before dispatch.
   - Final picked quantities must match the order lines or approved modification record.

3. **Support manifest readiness.**
   - Confirm product packages have assigned tracking identifiers.
   - Confirm destination license/address, product, quantity, planned departure time, planned arrival time, transporter, driver, and vehicle details are accurate.
   - Confirm printed transfer documentation is available to accompany transport when required.

4. **Monitor day-of-delivery exceptions.**
   - Common exceptions include late departure, customer not available, address issue, route change, package refusal, or partial rejection.
   - Log all issues and notify the accountable team.

5. **Confirm delivery outcome.**
   - Confirm delivered, partially delivered, rejected, or rescheduled status.
   - Confirm receiving action is complete where applicable.
   - Notify Accounting when order is ready for invoice posting or adjustment.

## Required Controls

- Order cannot ship with unresolved compliance hold.
- Manifest details must match planned delivery reality as closely as possible.
- Driver and transporter details must be reviewed before departure.
- Rejected packages must be documented and reconciled.
- Fulfillment quantity must match invoice quantity unless a documented adjustment exists.

## Delivery Exception Examples

| Exception | Immediate Action | Follow-Up |
|---|---|---|
| Customer closed / unavailable | Notify Sales Rep and Distribution | Reschedule or cancel with notes |
| Customer rejects SKU | Record rejection details | Coordinate inventory return and invoice adjustment |
| Quantity mismatch | Hold invoice if unresolved | Reconcile ERP, warehouse, and transfer record |
| Wrong ship-to address | Stop shipment if possible | Escalate to Compliance and Sales |
| Product not on order | Do not deliver extra product | Correct pick/pack issue and log root cause |
